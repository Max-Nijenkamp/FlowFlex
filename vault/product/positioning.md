# Market Positioning

---

## Target Customer

**Primary**: SMBs with 50–500 employees running 5–15 disconnected SaaS tools.

**Buying trigger**: The cost of context-switching, manual re-entry, and integration maintenance exceeds the cost of consolidation. Usually hits between 40–80 employees, or when a CFO reviews annual SaaS spend.

**Buyer**: CFO, COO, or Head of Operations. Champion: a department head (HR Manager, Finance Manager) tired of exporting CSVs.

**Not the target**:
- Enterprises (1,000+ employees) needing custom compliance or on-premise
- Solopreneurs / micro-businesses (free tools serve them fine)
- Companies already satisfied on a single ERP

---

## The Problem

Three compounding problems for SMBs with tool sprawl:

1. **App-switching** — approving leave means opening HR, cross-referencing a calendar, checking project load, notifying payroll manually
2. **Data re-entry** — one hire requires adding the employee to HR, payroll, IT provisioning, LMS, and project tools
3. **Integration sprawl** — Zapier/Make pipelines break when vendors change APIs; maintenance consumes engineering time

---

## Core Promise

One platform. All the modules a growing business needs. Activated one by one. Per-user per-module pricing.

No integrations to maintain, no data re-entry, no context-switching.

## Key Differentiator

**Modular SaaS**: Land with 1–2 modules, expand naturally. No lock-in, no bundle pressure.

Sales motion: land-and-expand. Start with HR Profiles + Leave. Add Finance Invoicing at month 6. Add CRM Pipeline when the sales team is hired.

---

## Strategic Notes

**WhatsApp (EU market)**: WhatsApp Business API is the #1 HubSpot complaint in DACH markets. Native WhatsApp in Communications positions FlowFlex strongly for EU SMBs.

**Vertical bundles** (marketing constructs, no new code):
- FlowFlex for Agencies — Projects + CRM + DMS + Finance
- FlowFlex for E-commerce — E-commerce + Marketing + CRM + Finance
- FlowFlex for Professional Services — Projects + Finance + CRM + Legal

**Usage-based pricing evolution**: high-volume modules (email sends, AI API calls, SMS) should move to consumption tiers rather than flat per-seat. Design data model to support this from the start.
